About

I am a Lecturer in Exercise Physiology at the University of Newcastle, Australia, with a PhD from the University of the Sunshine Coast (2023). My doctoral research explored how locomotor and collision workloads are quantified and their influence on fatigue in rugby football codes.

My current research program focuses on understanding and quantifying athlete workloads in team sports, examining how fatigue is measured in complex environments, and applying systems thinking and human factors methods to sport and exercise science. I maintain active national and international collaborations with applied sports science practitioners and research academics.

I am passionate about modern meta-science practices, including open science initiatives such as open access publishing and pre-registration. I am available for supervision of Honours, Masters, and PhD students.

In addition to academic research and teaching, I offer consultancy services in sports science, athlete monitoring, workload management, and performance analysis. I work with sporting organizations, teams, and individual practitioners to translate cutting-edge research into practical applications that improve athlete performance and reduce injury risk. For consultancy inquiries, please contact me via email.

Research Focus

My research explores the complex interplay between training load, competition demands, and athlete fatigue in team sports. Through a systems thinking approach, I investigate how external loads influence post-match recovery and performance in collision sports, particularly rugby league, rugby union, and Australian Rules football.

I am co-author of the book Systems Thinking Methods in Sport: Practical Guidance and Case Study Applications (Routledge, 2025), which provides a comprehensive guide to applying contemporary systems thinking methods in sport settings. The book demonstrates how to understand and optimize the behavior of complex systems inherent in sport performance, injury prevention, and team function.

Current projects include developing novel methodologies for quantifying collision dose, validating automated tackle detection systems, and identifying barriers to effective fatigue monitoring in professional rugby environments. My work emphasizes translating research findings into practical applications for coaches, practitioners, and athletes.
